## The Ancient Origins of Sharks

So, when did sharks first appear on the scene? Well, it's believed that the first shark-like creatures emerged during the Silurian period, around 416 million years ago. That's even before the first trees began to sprout on land! These early sharks were likely small, toothless creatures that fed on plankton and other tiny organisms. But over time, they evolved into the powerful predators we know and fear today.

### The Fossil Record

So, how do we know all this? Well, it's thanks to the amazing world of fossils. Scientists have discovered countless shark fossils from around the world, each one providing a glimpse into the evolutionary history of these incredible animals. For example, the 400-million-year-old fossil of a shark called Cladoselache has been found in North America, with a jaw full of razor-sharp teeth. Talk about a toothsome tale!

## The Long-Lived Sharks

But sharks aren't just a blast from the past – they're still thriving today. In fact, some species of sharks have been around for so long that they've seen the rise and fall of civilizations. The spined pygmy shark, for example, is believed to have been around since the time of the dinosaurs. And the whale shark? That gentle giant has been feeding on plankton for over 300 million years!

### The Longest-Lived Shark Species

One species that takes the cake for longevity is the Greenland shark. This massive predator has been swimming around since the last Ice Age, when the planet was a chilly 12°C (54°F). In fact, scientists have found a Greenland shark that's estimated to be around 400-500 years old. That's even older than some of the oldest human civilizations!
