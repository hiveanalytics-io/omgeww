We've Explored Less Than 20% of Earth's Oceans — Here's What Could Be Down There

Let's face it: the ocean is a mind-blowing, soul-stirring, and downright terrifying place. And yet, we've barely scratched the surface – literally. Despite making up 71% of our planet, the ocean remains one of the most under-explored and uncharted environments on Earth.

## The Unseen World

It's estimated that between 75% and 90% of all marine species remain undiscovered, with new species being discovered as frequently as every week. But what exactly lies beneath the waves, waiting to be uncovered? The truth is, we have no idea.

The deepest part of the ocean, the Challenger Deep in the Mariana Trench, is a staggering 36,000 feet (10,973 meters) below sea level. That's deeper than Mount Everest, the highest mountain on Earth, is tall. And yet, we've only just begun to explore the depths of the ocean.

## The Unseen Creatures

One of the most fascinating creatures that call the ocean home is the blobfish. This gelatinous fish has a face that's more suitable for a science experiment gone wrong than a marine animal. But despite its unusual appearance, the blobfish is a perfectly adapted creature, living at depths of up to 3,000 feet (914 meters) in the deep sea.

Another creature that's been discovered in the depths of the ocean is the anglerfish. These fish have a fleshy growth on their head that's used to lure prey – and it's also used as a beacon to attract mates. Talk about a weird courtship ritual!

## The Unseen Landscapes

But it's not just the creatures that are unknown – the landscapes themselves are also shrouded in mystery. The ocean floor is home to vast mountain ranges, deep trenches, and underwater volcanoes that are as active as they are hidden from view.

One of the most impressive features of the ocean floor is the Mid-Ocean Ridge, a vast underwater mountain range that stretches over 40,000 miles (64,000 kilometers) around the globe. This mountain range is so vast that it's visible from space – and yet, it's still one of the most under-explored regions of our planet.

## The Unseen Opportunities

So what can we learn from the ocean that we don't know yet? For one, the ocean holds the key to unlocking new medicines and treatments for some of the world's most deadly diseases. In fact, over 25% of all medicines are derived from marine organisms – and scientists estimate that there are hundreds of thousands of new species waiting to be discovered.

The ocean also holds the secrets of the Earth's climate – and understanding those secrets could be key to mitigating the effects of global warming. By studying the ocean's ecosystem and behavior, scientists may be able to unlock new technologies for carbon capture and storage, and other innovative solutions for a changing world.

## The Bottom Line

We've barely scratched the surface of the ocean – and that's a pretty big deal. The ocean is a vast, mysterious, and awe-inspiring environment that holds the key to unlocking new discoveries, new technologies, and new ways of understanding our planet. So next time you're at the beach or by the ocean, remember: there's still so much we don't know – and that's what makes it so bloody fascinating.
