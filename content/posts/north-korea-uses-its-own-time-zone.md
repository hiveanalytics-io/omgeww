## The Unconventional Timekeeper

In 2018, North Korea officially adopted a new time zone, known as Pyongyang Time (PST), which is 30 minutes ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). But what's the reasoning behind this bold move? ## A History of Defiance

North Korea's history of being a contrarian nation dates back to the 1950s, when it first adopted its own time zone – Pyongyang Time, back then. The country's original time zone was 21 degrees 50 minutes east of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), but it was adjusted to 105 degrees east in 1959, which is why it's now 30 minutes ahead of UTC.

## The World's Most Isolated Time Zone

North Korea's time zone is so unique that it's often referred to as the world's most isolated time zone. According to the International Organization for Standardization (ISO), North Korea's time zone is not recognized by any other country, making it one of the few time zones in the world without an official designation.

So, why did North Korea adopt its own time zone? One theory is that it's an attempt to reclaim control over its own destiny, rather than following the lead of other countries. By doing so, North Korea is able to differentiate itself from the rest of the world and assert its independence.
