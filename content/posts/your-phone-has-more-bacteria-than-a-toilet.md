## The Toilet Truth
Toilet bowls are notoriously dirty, but they can't hold a candle to the bacteria found on your phone. In fact, a study published in the journal Science in 2019 found that the average smartphone contains around 1.25 million times more bacteria than a toilet.

So, what's the most common bacteria found on a phone? It's not the usual suspects like E. coli or Salmonella. No, the top spot goes to a bacteria called Staphylococcus epidermidis. This bacteria is usually found on the skin, but it can also thrive on phone screens, keyboards, and other surfaces.

## The Number of Bacteria on a Phone
But just how many bacteria are we talking about here? In 2018, a team of researchers from the University of Wisconsin-Madison discovered that the average smartphone contained around 10 million bacteria per square inch. That's more than the number of bacteria found in a typical toilet bowl, which is estimated to contain around 3.5 million bacteria per square inch.

## The Source of the Bacteria
So, where is all this bacteria coming from? It's likely from the people you touch, the surfaces you wipe, and the air you breathe. Your phone is a breeding ground for bacteria because it's always in your pocket or purse, and it's constantly exposed to your skin oils and other contaminants.

## The Consequences of Bacterial Overload
Having a phone with more bacteria than a toilet is more than just gross - it's also a health risk. When you touch your face, you're transferring bacteria from your phone to your eyes, nose, and mouth. This can lead to a range of problems, from colds and flu to more serious infections like MRSA.

## The Solution
So, what can you do to clean your phone and reduce the number of bacteria on it? The answer is simple: wash it regularly. Use a disinfectant wipe or a solution of equal parts water and white vinegar to kill the bacteria. You can also try using a phone sanitizer, but be sure to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your phone.
