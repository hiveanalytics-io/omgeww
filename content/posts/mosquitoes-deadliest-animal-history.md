Mosquitoes Have Killed More Humans Than Every War in History Combined

Imagine a world without mosquitoes, a world where the dreaded itchy bite of the infamous insect is a distant memory. Sounds like a utopia, right? But what if we told you that mosquitoes have been responsible for more human deaths than every war in history combined? Yes, you read that right – the tiny, bloodthirsty insect has claimed more lives than the sum of all wars throughout human history.

## The Unstoppable Killing Machine

It's estimated that mosquitoes are responsible for over one million deaths per year, according to the World Health Organization (WHO). This staggering number is more than the total number of deaths attributed to all wars from ancient times to the present day. That's right, folks, mosquitoes have been the ultimate killing machine for thousands of years, and we're still feeling their sting today.

### The Plague of Malaria

One of the most significant mosquito-borne diseases is malaria. This deadly illness has been responsible for an estimated 435 million deaths since 1900, according to the WHO. That's more than the total number of people who died in the Second World War. Malaria is spread by the Anopheles mosquito, which is found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. In fact, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), there were over 228 million reported cases of malaria in 2019 alone.

### The Forgotten Killer: Dengue Fever

While malaria gets most of the attention, another mosquito-borne disease, dengue fever, is just as deadly. According to the WHO, dengue fever is responsible for over 390,000 deaths per year, mostly in Asia and Latin America. This disease is spread by the Aedes mosquito, which is also found in tropical and subtropical regions. In fact, the CDC reports that between 2001 and 2010, there were over 2.5 million reported cases of dengue fever in the United States alone.

### The Global Impact

Mosquitoes aren't just limited to tropical regions; they can be found in almost every corner of the globe. In fact, the CDC reports that mosquitoes are responsible for the transmission of diseases in over 100 countries. The impact of mosquitoes goes beyond just human lives; they also affect the economy and the environment. According to a study published in the journal PLOS Neglected Tropical Diseases, mosquitoes cost the global economy over $12 billion per year in lost productivity and healthcare costs.
