### A Brief History of Urine in Ancient Rome

In ancient Rome, urine was considered a valuable resource. It was used not only as a source of wealth but also as a treatment for a range of ailments, including bad breath. ### The Roman Formula for Fresh Breath

The Roman naturalist Pliny the Elder wrote extensively on the use of urine as a medicine. In his book "Naturalis Historia," he described a mixture of urine, vinegar, and powdered bones as a mouthwash that could cure bad breath. The recipe, known as "saliva," was likely used by the wealthy and elite, who could afford to collect and process their own urine.
