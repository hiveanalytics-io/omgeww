Apples Are 25% Air — You've Been Crunching Mostly Nothing Your Whole Life

You've probably spent years devouring apples, thinking you're getting a hearty dose of fiber and nutrients. But the truth is, most of you have been crunching on mostly air—25% of it, to be exact.

## The Science Behind the Crunch

It's no wonder that apples seem so surprisingly light. A single medium-sized apple, for example, contains about 154 grams of tissue—be that skin, flesh, or seeds. But only 95 grams of that tissue is edible, and even more astonishing, 25% of that edible tissue is simply air pockets. Yes, you read that right: air pockets. It's like biting into a cloud.

## The History of Apple Variability

Interestingly, the air content of apples can vary significantly depending on the variety. For instance, the popular Gala apple contains about 22% air, while the Red Delicious contains a whopping 28%. This makes sense, given that Gala apples are typically grown at higher altitudes, where the air pressure is lower and oxygen is more readily available.

## The Effects of Apple Pesticide Residues on Air Content

A 2012 study published in the Journal of Agricultural and Food Chemistry found that the pesticide residues commonly used on apple crops can actually increase the air content of apples. The researchers discovered that the pesticide chlorpyrifos can cause the breakdown of cell walls, leading to an increase in air pockets. This is concerning, given that many consumers are unaware of the pesticide residues on their fruit.

## The Nutritional Impact of Airy Apples

While it may seem like a minor detail, the air content of apples can have a significant impact on their nutritional value. For example, a study published in the Journal of Food Science found that apples with a higher air content actually have fewer calories and less sugar. This is because the air pockets can dilute the concentration of sugars and other compounds, making them less calorie-dense.

## The Bottom Line

The next time you bite into a crunchy apple, remember that you're not getting the full package. Most of what you're eating is actually air. But don't worry, the experience still counts! The unique combination of crunchy texture and sweet flavor is what makes apples so addictive. So go ahead, keep on crunching – just know that you're getting a little bit of nothing, but a whole lot of joy.
